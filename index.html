• Who
  • What
  • Where
  • When
  • Why

Who

Who refers to the people, characters, or entities involved in a situation. This could include individuals, organizations, or even abstract concepts (like "nature" or "technology").
Example: In a story about climate change, "Who" might be governments, scientists, or communities affected by it.

Guiding Questions:

  • Who are the key players in this scenario?
  • Are there multiple groups involved?
  • What roles do they play?

What

What describes the main action, event, or topic. It answers "what happened?" or "what is the focus?"
Example: If the topic is a business, "What" could be "launching a new product."

Guiding Questions:

  • What is the central event or action?
  • Is it a problem, a solution, or a process?
  • What outcome is expected?

Where

Where specifies the location, time, or context. It answers "where did this happen?" or "in what context?"
Example: For a historical event, "Where" might be "Paris during the French Revolution."

Guiding Questions:

  • Where geographically or virtually did this occur?
  • What time period or era is relevant?
  • Is there a symbolic location?

When

When answers "when did this happen?" or "when is this relevant?" It includes timeframes, deadlines, or schedules.
Example: For a project, "When" could be "by December 2026."

Guiding Questions:

  • What is the timeline for this event?
  • Are there specific deadlines?
  • Is timing critical to the outcome?

Why

Why explains the reason or purpose behind something. It answers "why does it matter?" or "what is the goal?"
Example: For a charity event, "Why" might be "to raise funds for education."

Guiding Questions:

  • What is the underlying motivation?
  • How does this connect to larger goals?
  • What would happen without this reason?
